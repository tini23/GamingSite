Pros & Cons

Pros

  • Raw power: The Xbox Series X is the most powerful console in terms of raw performance, with better graphics and faster load times compared to the PS5.
  • Game Pass: Xbox Game Pass offers access to hundreds of games, including day-one releases from Xbox Game Studios, making it an incredible value for gamers.
  • Backward compatibility: The Series X supports thousands of Xbox One, Xbox 360, and original Xbox games, making it easy to carry over your old game library.
  • Quick Resume: This feature allows you to switch between multiple games instantly without needing to restart, saving you valuable time.
  • Affordable storage expansion: With the Seagate Storage Expansion Card, you can easily add 1TB of storage, matching the internal SSD’s speed.

Cons

  • Lack of exclusive titles: While Xbox Game Pass offers great value, Xbox’s first-party exclusive titles aren’t as numerous or renowned as PlayStation’s.
  • Design: The Series X is large and boxy, which may not appeal to everyone in terms of aesthetic and fitting into entertainment setups.
  • Limited VR support: Unlike PlayStation, Xbox doesn’t have a VR system (though Microsoft has indicated interest in the future).

Accessories

Xbox Wireless Controller

A refined version of the previous controller with improved ergonomics and better connectivity.

Xbox Elite Wireless Controller Series 2

A premium controller with customizable features for serious gamers.

Xbox Adaptive Controller

Designed for gamers with limited mobility, this controller features large programmable buttons and connects to external devices like switches, buttons, mounts, and joysticks to create a custom controller experience.

Xbox Wireless Headset

Delivers high-quality audio and chat with auto-mute and voice isolation. It supports spatial sound technologies like Windows Sonic, Dolby Atmos, and DTS Headphone:X.
